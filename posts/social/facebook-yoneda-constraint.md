Here's something that changed how we think about four completely different problems.

What do these have in common?

1) Godel proved math can't prove all truths about itself (1931)
2) Quantum physics can't explain why measurement "collapses" a wave function
3) A compiler that compiles itself always needs a starting point it can't verify
4) An AI can't fully verify that it's safe

The answer: they are all the same problem wearing different costumes.

We wrote a 23-page proof showing that one axiom from category theory (a branch of mathematics) explains all four. The axiom is called The Yoneda Constraint, and it says:

No system that is part of a larger structure can build a complete picture of that structure from the inside.

Think of it like trying to see your own eyes without a mirror. You are part of the system you are trying to observe. There will always be a gap.

For AI, this matters right now. It means an AI system cannot fully certify its own safety — not because our technology is not good enough, but because mathematics says it is impossible. That does not mean AI safety is hopeless. It means self-certification is the wrong strategy. External checks, layered oversight, partial guarantees composed together — those paths remain open.

One axiom. Four problems. One proof.
