# Twitter Thread: JAPL

## Tweet 1
We built a programming language from scratch.

7 research papers. 160+ pages. A self-hosting compiler. Dual backends (TypeScript + C). 251 tests. A proof-of-concept distributed app.

Here's the story of JAPL.

## Tweet 2
The design: take the best ideas from four languages.

- Erlang's processes and fault tolerance
- Rust's ownership and resource safety
- Go's simplicity and readability
- TypeScript's ecosystem reach

Pure by default. Concurrent by design. Resource-safe by construction.

## Tweet 3
The compiler: written in TypeScript, targeting both TS and C.

Lexer, parser, type checker, code generator — all from scratch. 251 tests covering every stage. No parser generators. No shortcuts.

## Tweet 4
The proof: JAPL compiles itself.

1,495 lines of JAPL compiling JAPL. A self-hosting compiler that validates the language is expressive enough to build its own toolchain.

The bootstrap paradox is real — and we wrote a paper about that too.

## Tweet 5
The app: TimeTracker.

A distributed time-tracking application built in JAPL with supervisor trees, fault-tolerant processes, and hot code reloading. Not a toy — a proof that the language works for real systems.
